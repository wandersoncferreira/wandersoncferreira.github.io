On Abstraction

May 30, 2020
clojure design

Youtube

Speaker

Zach Tellman

Thoughts

Difficult presentation because it deals with some very conceptual topics, but Zach is very knowledgeable about his presentations there are a lot of research behind it and we can tell how much effort he made to deliver a good material and relevant.

“The fundamental question of software is how do we deal with change”

I am going to watch this over and over, but I also need to read all the material he suggested.

Quotes

Abstraction maps the internal model onto external semantics.

“To think is to forget a difference, to generalize, to abstract. In the overly replete world of Funes there were nothing but details, almost contiguous details.”

“An abstraction is useful only if its assumptions are sound”

If an abstraction can’t be discarded, it becomes coercive. What it doesnt see might disapper.

Software rould be easy, if it weren’t for changing environments. No one tries to add a carwash to a bridge when it’s halfway built.

Blind optimism isn’t a substitute for understanding.

Recommendations

  1. Our models should be simplest thing that satisfices often enough
  2. When assumptions leak out, we have to use conventions
  3. Starting from scratch can be expensive
  4. Assumptions that fail together belong together

Books

  2. Proof of Correctness of Data Representations - by Hoare
  3. The structure of scientific revolutions - by Thomas Kuhn
  4. The sciences of the Artificial - by Herbert A. Simon
  5. Funes the memorious - by Jorge Luis Borges
  6. Simulacra and Simulation - by Jean Baudrillard
  7. Seeing like a state - by James Scott
  8. Notes on the synthesis of form - by Christopher Alexander
